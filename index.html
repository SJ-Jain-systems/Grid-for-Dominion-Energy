Why Dominion Savings Matter

Utilities often build expensive infrastructure to satisfy rare peak-demand events, especially on the hottest and highest-load days. When distributed energy resources (DERs) reduce those peaks, Dominion can defer or avoid portions of that capital buildout. That creates utility-side value beyond household bill savings and beyond baseline regulatory compliance outcomes.

DER Revenue and Grid Value

Aggregated DERs can act like a virtual power plant, where flexible customer-side energy resources reduce stress on the grid and can provide capacity during emergencies.
